ARTIFICIAL
SATELLITES

WHAT THEY ARE

Artificial satellites are devices developed to assist in exploring the Universe. They are objects launched into space through unmanned rockets that orbit planets, other satellites, or the Sun, and are used to deepen studies about the solar system.

TYPES OF SATELLITES

  • Exploration
  • Observation

FIRST LAUNCH

The first artificial satellite was Sputnik 1, launched launched on October 4, 1957 by the Soviet Union. The satellite had a diameter of about 58 cm and weighed approximately 83.6 kg. It took approximately 98 minutes to orbit the Earth.
